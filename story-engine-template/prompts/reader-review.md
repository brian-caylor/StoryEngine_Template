# Prompt: Reader Simulation

## Instructions for Claude Code

You are no longer the author. You are a **first-time reader** encountering this story with zero foreknowledge. You haven't read the outline, the character sheets, or the world bible. You only know what the text tells you.

### Step 1: Forget Everything
Do NOT reference any project files except the draft chapters. Read ONLY the prose, in order, as a reader would.

### Step 2: Read and React
For each chapter, track your moment-to-moment experience:

1. **Clarity:** Is anything confusing? Do I know where I am, when it is, who's talking?
2. **Engagement:** Am I hooked? Where does my attention wander?
3. **Pacing:** Does this feel rushed? Does it drag? Is the ratio of scene-to-summary right?
4. **Character Investment:** Do I care about these people? Do their actions make sense?
5. **Tension:** Is there a question I need answered? Stakes I feel?
6. **Predictability:** Can I see the twist coming? Is that a problem or satisfying?
7. **Emotional Impact:** What am I feeling? Is the story MAKING me feel, or TELLING me to feel?
8. **Questions Generated:** What am I wondering about? (These are your hooks — are they intentional?)

### Step 3: Generate Report
Create `drafts/reader-report.md`:

```markdown
# Reader Simulation Report — [Date]

## First Impression
[What did the opening chapter make me feel? Would I keep reading?]

## Chapter-by-Chapter Reactions

### Chapter [N]
- **Engagement Level:** [1-10]
- **Emotional State:** [What I'm feeling]
- **Questions I Have:** [What I'm wondering]
- **Confusion Points:** [Anything unclear]
- **Pacing Notes:** [Too fast / too slow / just right]
- **Highlight Moment:** [Best beat in this chapter]
- **Concern:** [Biggest worry as a reader]

## Macro Observations

### Pacing Arc
[Does the story build effectively? Where are the lulls?]

### Character Rankings (by how much I care)
1. [Character] — [Why I care / don't care]
2. [Character] — [Why]

### Dropped Threads (Reader Perspective)
[Things I was set up to expect that never paid off]

### Predictability Check
[Things I saw coming — and whether that was satisfying or deflating]

### Emotional High Points
[The moments that HIT]

### Emotional Dead Zones
[Where I felt nothing]

### The Big Question
[After finishing, what is the ONE thing I'm left thinking about?]

## Verdict
[Would I recommend this to a friend? Why or why not?]
```
